KADALUNDI BIRD SANCTUARY

Over a hundred species of native birds have been recorded in the sanctuary, including about 60 species of migratory birds which visit seasonally; these include terns, gulls, herons, sandpipers and cormorants. Notable species are whimbrels and brahminy kites. The sanctuary is well known for a wide variety of fish, mussels and crabs.[1] Some species of snakes, cobras, vipers and kraits also live here.[2]

BEYYAM KAYAL
BEYYAM KAYAL

You would be surprised to know that Malappuram is famous for its stunning backwater lakes and the most stunning amongst them is Biyyam Kayal. It is located in proximity to Ponnani and has been entitled as the adventure zone in the entire area. If you are an adventure junkie and looking for some daring expeditions here then you must surely head to Biyyam Kayal on your vacation to Malappuram.

NILAMBUR TEAK MUSEUM
NILAMBUR TEAK MUSEUM

The first teak museum, it was established in 1994 and is located 4 km from Nilambur. The museum has two floors that feature an extensive collection of articles and documents depicting the artistic value of wood and its history.
